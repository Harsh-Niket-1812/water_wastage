Causes of Water Pollution

Water pollution is caused by harmful chemicals and biological contaminants being released into bodies of water. Major causes include:

  • Industrial waste
  • Agricultural runoff (pesticides, fertilizers)
  • Oil spills
  • Plastic and other solid waste
  • Sewage and wastewater
  • Mining activities

Effects of Water Pollution

Water pollution has serious consequences for human health, aquatic life, and ecosystems:

  • Waterborne diseases such as cholera and dysentery
  • Destruction of aquatic ecosystems
  • Contaminated drinking water supplies
  • Eutrophication, causing algal blooms and oxygen depletion
  • Damage to marine life due to plastic ingestion and toxic waste

How to Reduce Water Pollution

There are several actions that can be taken to reduce water pollution:

  • Proper treatment of industrial and sewage waste
  • Reducing the use of harmful pesticides and fertilizers
  • Cleaning up oil spills and controlling plastic waste
  • Implementing stronger water protection policies
  • Promoting water conservation and recycling
